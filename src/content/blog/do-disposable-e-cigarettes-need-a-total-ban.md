As the overwhelming popularity of disposable e-cigarettes is relatively engulfed worldwide, but with that comes a lot of naysayers, so do we need to go all out and ban the production, sale, and advertising of disposable puffs?

The Australian government has taken action to implement a comprehensive ban on the import of all disposable electronic cigarettes, whether containing nicotine or not, as well as non-therapeutic electronic cigarettes. As the first country to enforce a ban on disposable e-cigarettes in 2024, Australia demonstrates its high regard for public health and its steadfast commitment to preventing youth vaping.

The government has condemned them as providing addictive recreational products for children, arguing that they have a disproportionate impact on young people.

Mark Butler, the Minister of Health said the move was aimed at reversing a “worrying” rise in the use of e-cigarettes among young people. The government also mentioned that more forbidding regulations would be introduced on manufacture, advertising and supply of disposable e-cigarettes in 2024.

The ban is highly supported by the Australian Medical Association. “Australia has been at the forefront of the world’s efforts to reduce smoking rates and the health problems they cause, so the government’s decision to act decisively to stop e-cigarettes contributing to the further spread of the problem is broadly welcomed,” said Australian Medical Association President Steve Robson.

However, it is worth noting that e-cigarettes are indeed a product that can assist some smokers who want to quit to transition to smoking cessation. As a result, the government has also announced a plan to allow doctors and nurses to prescribe e-cigarettes to patients from January 1 next year, where clinically applicable. This decision is intended to balance the public health benefits with the needs of individuals.

In summary, we can now easily imagine that some of the disposable e-cigarette enthusiasts are already stocking up.

## Are disposable e-cigarettes really so unforgivable?

Compared to traditional cigarettes, e-cigarettes are significantly less harmful to the human body. Many researches and papers have revealed that e-cigarettes have an advantageous effect in helping smokers to quit smoking. As a result, the UK government also announced in April of this year, a program named **Smoke Replacement to Quit** will provide free Vape starter kits to about 1 million smokers.

Code-compliant e-cigarettes offer an alternative to traditional tobacco for those who want to quit smoking, as well as a viable option for those who want to reduce the amount of cigarettes they smoke. The use of e-cigarettes can gradually reduce nicotine dependence and ultimately lead to smoking cessation. As the popularity of e-cigarettes gradually increases, more and more people are beginning to recognize their advantages over traditional cigarettes, while e-cigarettes are not completely risk-free, they do offer a healthier and more viable alternative for smokers.

With the development of the e-cigarette industry, disposable puffs as one of the e-cigarettes types are rising rapidly. These disposable e-cigarette products are fanciful and attract young people due to their beautiful design, variety, rich flavors, and unique fashion elements. As disposable e-cigarettes are a blast worldwide, following the hot issue about the product’s safety and health effects. and the discussion on how to prevent underage use of disposable has become an increasingly pressing issue.

Some studies have shown that some young people are likely to use disposable e-cigarettes because of their stylish appearance and wide range of flavors, their increasing nicotine craving. In addition, the environmental damage contributed by disposable e-cigarettes has become an increasingly serious problem, which has accumulated a great deal of controversy.

In order to solve these problems, the government and the community should adopt stricter regulatory measures. The government should formulate technical standards and publish laws and regulations to comprehensively regulate the production, sale and use of disposable e-cigarettes. At the same time, schools and parents should also strengthen education and guidance for minors, so that they can understand the hazards and risks of disposable e-cigarettes and avoid them becoming victims of e-cigarettes.

So all in all it’s not the disposable e-cigarette products that are the problem, it’s the enforcement of the law and how to improve it: banning the sale of disposable e-cigarettes to young people under the age of 18.
