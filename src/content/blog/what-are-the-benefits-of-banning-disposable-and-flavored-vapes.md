Amidst an increasingly strengthen global anti-smoking atmosphere and a rising public health consciousness, the concept of *** Harm Reduction *** has gradually become a common aspiration in the international community and the tobacco industry. When we talk about “tobacco control,” essentially, we are struggling to reduce the dual burden that tobacco imposes on social health and the economy. By advocating and implementing tobacco control measures, we hope to reduce the health risks associated with tobacco and reduce the economic losses caused by smoking, promoting a healthier and more sustainable society.

The enactment of bans on disposable electronic cigarettes appears to be on the rise, However, these countries seemingly are not conducting comprehensive and precise risk assessments on the bans. **Taking the UK as an example, its most proud smoking cessation strategies have been hastily abandoned after the imposition of bans on disposable electronic cigarettes and flavor bans.** According to a recent survey by Evapo (a leading online electronic cigarette retailer in the UK), as the bans are gradually implemented, there is a high potential that former electronic cigarette users will revert to traditional tobacco products. Additionally, the World Vapers’ Alliance (WVA) out that electronic cigarettes reduce harm by up to 95% compared to traditional tobacco and are far more effective than traditional methods in helping people quit smoking. Moreover, WVA Director Michael Landl emphasizes that such restrictions overlook the scientific evidence of electronic cigarettes in assisting smoking cessation, and flavored electronic cigarettes can increase the success rate of quitting smoking by an astonishing 230%.

In the United States, Governor Ron DeSantis of Florida also opposed the ban. He firmly believes that flavored electronic cigarettes play a positive role in helping smokers quit, and therefore, an outright ban should not be hastily imposed.

As individuals who smoke, we may all feel confused and concerned: **is it really appropriate for the government to push forward with the implementation of bans without clear results?** Shouldn’t local authorities conduct a rigorous risk assessment before making decisions? Especially in discussions about further restrictions on electronic cigarettes (such as flavor bans), should we not be more cautions about evaluating the potential impact of these policies on public health? After all, the adoption of any policy should be based on thoughtful consideration and broad deliberation of the public interest, rather than blindly forging ahead.

## Is the ban on disposable electronic cigarettes/flavors really a wrong decision?

In fact, there is a gradual decline in the acceptance of traditional cigarettes. Besides electronic cigarettes, there are many other new tobacco products on the market as alternatives. According to recent data, the number of nicotine pouches users in the EU soared to 2.6 million in 2023, an astonishing increase of 7% compared to the previous year. This growth trend is also evident in other countries, and undoubtedly demonstrates the increasing consumer interest and acceptance of these new products, which claim to reduce the harm of traditional cigarettes.

However, which product has the lowest risk is still an undecided question.

In the future, we may see more bans and control measures on various new tobacco products. At that time, what should we do, is the electronic cigarette really as unbearable as some people say, these are questions worth pondering.

**Why ban disposable electronic cigarettes? ** The main objective of the ban on disposable electronic cigarettes and flavors is to address the increasing number of young people using electronic cigarettes and to protect children’s health and environmental sustainability. The nicotine content in electronic cigarettes is highly addictive, and prolonged exposure to it may have long-term negative effects on the brain development of adolescents and children. Even worse, the intermittent use of electronic cigarettes significantly increases the likelihood that children and adolescents will switch to traditional cigarettes in the future. **Why ban flavored electronic cigarettes?**

The main purpose of restricting the flavors of electronic cigarettes is to protect minors and prevent them from being tempted by electronic cigarettes. by the variety of flavors of electronic cigarettes. Currently, only about 5% of electronic cigarette products on the market are tobacco flavored, the remaining 95% are various flavors. To address this issue, the United States implemented a ban on electronic cigarette flavors in 2019, only tobacco and menthol flavors allowed, aimed at reducing the adolescents’ access and use of electronic cigarettes for their health and safety.

Given this, children’s exposure to electronic cigarette products is completely intolerant. However, there are many electronic cigarette products on the market that are clearly designed to attract the attention of young people, such as those that resemble milk tea cups or toy-shaped electronic cigarette devices. **The appearance of these products undoubtedly exacerbates the risk of adolescent electronic cigarette use. **## Benefits of banning disposable electronic cigarettes and flavored electronic cigarettes: ** Benefit one: **Due to the stern prohibitions on selling channels of disposable electronic cigarettes and flavored electronic cigarettes to minors, the exposure of young people to electronic cigarettes is greatly reduced.. This undoubtedly curbs the trend of young people smoking electronic cigarettes, reduces the number of young smokers, and also reduces the number of initial smokers. ** Benefit two:**

Reducing environmental pollution while alleviating the pressure to deal with waste and waste of scarce resources.

Disposable electronic cigarettes are favored for their practicality, convenience, and disposability. However, this convenience has triggered numerous serious environmental problems. Many countries and regions are facing the challenge of how to handle a large number of discarded electronic cigarette devices. A survey by a British environmental charity showed that these disposable electronic cigarette wastes not only seriously threaten the survival environment of wildlife but also pollute our streets, parks, and beaches. Worst of all, up to 260 million lithium batteries are thrown away indiscriminately every year, not only wasting valuable and scarce resources, but also putting a heavy burden on the environment.
